Tai Yang Greater Yang

B1 Thiamine Exterior Defense • Glycolysis
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B1 (Thiamine) as TPP is essential for pyruvate dehydrogenase, linking glycolysis to TCA cycle. Critical for energy production in high-demand tissues (brain, heart, muscle).
Treatment Principle: Release the Exterior (解表), induce sweating. Priority 1 - Always treat first if present. B1 supports defensive qi and acetylcholine synthesis for neuromuscular function.

Yang Ming Bright Yang

B3 Niacin Interior Heat • NAD+/NADP+
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B3 (Niacin) forms NAD+/NADP+, central to cellular respiration, TCA cycle, and ATP production. The "Four Bigs" reflect intense metabolic demand and heat generation.
Treatment Principle: Clear Heat (清热), drain downward if necessary. Priority 2 - Treat after Tai Yang if both present. B3 supports intense metabolic heat and cellular energy demands.

Shao Yang Lesser Yang

B2 Riboflavin Half-Exterior/Half-Interior • FAD/FMN
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B2 (Riboflavin) forms FAD/FMN cofactors for mitochondrial energy generation and oxidation-reduction reactions. The pivot mechanism reflects redox balance between exterior and interior.
Treatment Principle: Harmonize Shao Yang (和解少阳). Priority 3 - Cannot use sweating or purging. B2 supports the pivot mechanism and redox balance between Biao and Li.

Tai Yin Greater Yin

B5 Pantothenic Interior Cold/Deficiency • Acetyl-CoA
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B5 (Pantothenic Acid) forms Coenzyme A, essential for acetyl-CoA synthesis. Critical for fatty acid metabolism, steroid synthesis, and adrenal function. Supports Spleen's transformation/transportation.
Treatment Principle: Warm the Middle (温中), strengthen Spleen Yang. Priority 4 - First Yin stage to treat. B5 supports digestive fire and adrenal yang (ming men).

Shao Yin Lesser Yin

B9 Folate B12 Cobalamin Deep Deficiency • Methylation
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B9 (Folate) & B12 (Cobalamin) drive methylation cycles, DNA synthesis, and red blood cell formation. Critical for Heart-Kidney axis, neurological function, and essence (jing) preservation.
Treatment Principle: Rescue Yang (回阳) or Nourish Yin (滋阴). Priority 5 - Deep deficiency requires careful tonification. B9/B12 support blood and essence production. Avoid sweating/purging.

Jue Yin Terminal Yin

B6 Pyridoxine Mixed Heat/Cold • Transamination
0
Points
Metabolic Function: B6 (Pyridoxine) as PLP is essential for amino acid metabolism, neurotransmitter synthesis (serotonin, dopamine, GABA), and glycogen phosphorylase. Regulates the complex mixed patterns of terminal stage.
Treatment Principle: Clear Upper Heat, Warm Lower Cold (清上温下). Priority 6 - Final stage requiring complex balancing. B6 regulates neurotransmitter balance and mixed metabolic states.
